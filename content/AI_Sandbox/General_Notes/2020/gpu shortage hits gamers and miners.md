# gpu shortage hits gamers and miners

Nvidia's RTX 30-series sold out in minutes as remote workers and crypto miners snapped up every chip, turning GPU availability into a national news item ([Reuters GPU Shortage](https://www.reuters.com/article/us-nvidia-chips/nvidia-gpus-sell-out-as-crypto-miners-remote-workers-drive-demand-idUSKBN27W0XH)). The shortage underscored how hardware supply chains respond to multipole demand.

## signal braid
- Retailers rationed cards, showing that demand curves for entertainment and productivity align.
- Miners using GPUs for Ethereum added unpredictable capacity shifts, echoing the speculation in [[three arrows liquidation exposes leverage maze]].
- Supply chain constraints also mirrored the logistic knits from [[container rates collapse as demand evaporates]].

## risk surface
- High prices push builders toward custom ASICs, reshaping the hardware market.
- Secondary markets flourish, so warranties and reliability degrade.
- Nvidia might throttle sales to miners, but such policies are hard to enforce.

## linkage anchor
This shortage ties to [[nvidia ampere gpu reveal tightens datacenter race]] because Ampere supply is finite, and it links to [[telehealth surge rewrites medical delivery]] since remote work adds to demand.

## my take
When so many demanding workloads collide, shortages happen quickly. Companies now have to think of GPUs as shared infrastructure rather than consumer gadgets.
