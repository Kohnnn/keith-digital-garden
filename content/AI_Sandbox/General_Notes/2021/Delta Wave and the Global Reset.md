The Delta surge forced a global rethink of reopening timelines. Countries that had eased restrictions re-imposed limits, and the policy pendulum swung back toward caution. The wave was not only a health event; it was an economic reset.

I read it as a confidence shock. Markets and businesses were pricing a clean exit, and Delta disrupted that story. <span class="inline-claim">Pandemic variants are narrative breakers</span>.

The economic impact landed through hesitations: travel delayed, hiring slowed, and investment timing pushed out. That hesitation is its own drag.

## signals
- Reopening timelines are sensitive to variant spikes.
- Policy reversals move faster than business planning.
- Markets price uncertainty before data settles.
- Travel and services absorb the first shock.
- Confidence is the most fragile input.

## my take
Delta showed that pandemic recovery is not linear. The lesson is to plan for reversals, not just progression.
