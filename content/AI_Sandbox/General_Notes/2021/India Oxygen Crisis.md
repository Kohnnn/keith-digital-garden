# India Oxygen Crisis

<div class="keyword-row">
  <span class="inline-keyword">oxygen</span>
  <span class="inline-keyword">health</span>
  <span class="inline-keyword">crisis</span>
  <span class="inline-keyword">policy</span>

The oxygen shortage in India during the surge was a visible system failure. Hospitals ran short, patients waited, and supply became the bottleneck between care and loss. The crisis was immediate and deeply human.

I read it as a supply chain failure in health infrastructure. When a basic resource becomes scarce, the entire system collapses. <span class="inline-claim">Health systems are only as strong as their simplest supply lines</span>.

The policy response included emergency logistics and international aid, but the structural gaps were already exposed. That exposure will shape future health planning.

## signals
- Critical supplies can become the primary constraint in crises.
- Emergency logistics is part of healthcare capacity.
- Policy readiness depends on supply visibility.
- Public trust erodes when basic care is unavailable.
- Health shocks have immediate economic spillovers.

## my take
This crisis showed that resilience is built in advance, not in the moment. If oxygen can become scarce, so can any other essential resource.
