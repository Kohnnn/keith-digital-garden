Shifts in mask guidance tested public trust. When rules changed, compliance became less about science and more about confidence in institutions. The speed of the shifts mattered as much as the content.

I read it as a fatigue signal. People could accept strict rules, but they needed consistency. <span class="inline-claim">Policy clarity is part of public health</span>.

The economic layer is indirect but real: unclear guidance slows reopening decisions and complicates workplace policy.

## signals
- Frequent shifts reduce compliance over time.
- Trust depends on clarity and consistency.
- Policy changes ripple into workplace rules.
- Public fatigue is a measurable risk.
- Health messaging competes with social narratives.

## my take
This was a case study in communication. The science changed, but the delivery determined the public response.

I keep this linked to [[Vaccine Mandates Expand]] because both are about how policy messaging shapes compliance.
