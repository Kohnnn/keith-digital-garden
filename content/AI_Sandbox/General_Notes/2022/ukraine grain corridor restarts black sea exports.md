# ukraine grain corridor restarts black sea exports

Ukraine, Russia, Turkey, and the UN signed a deal in Istanbul to restart grain exports from Odesa, Chornomorsk, and Yuzhny, unlocking millions of tons of wheat and corn stuck since the invasion ([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/europe/un-russia-ukraine-sign-deal-restart-grain-export-2022-07-22/)). A joint coordination center now inspects ships.

## scene cut
The agreement creates safe corridors and guarantees demining along specific lanes. Insurance and freight costs remain elevated, but the first convoys started loading within days, signaling to importers that supplies might normalize.

## constraint map
- Ships still require inspections in Istanbul, adding time and bureaucracy.
- Any missile strike on the participating ports could void the deal.
- Rail and road alternatives cannot scale fast enough if the corridor shuts again.
