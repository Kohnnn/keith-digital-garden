# context permission maps become standard in onboarding

Organizations are formalizing context permission maps during onboarding to define who can access which retrieval scopes and tool actions from day one ([CISA identity guidance](https://www.cisa.gov/topics/cybersecurity-best-practices/identity-and-access-management)).

*see also:* [[identity graph cleanup becomes prerequisite for copilots]] · [[tenant scoped memory stores reduce cross account leakage]]

## why this matters
Early permission mapping reduces accidental privilege spread as teams scale agent use.

## operating signal
- Fewer cross-team access incidents during rollout.
- Faster security review for new workflows.
- Cleaner escalation when permission boundaries are explicit.

## my take
Permission maps are becoming the onboarding equivalent of architecture diagrams.
