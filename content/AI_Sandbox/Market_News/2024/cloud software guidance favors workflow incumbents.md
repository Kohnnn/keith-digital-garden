# cloud software guidance favors workflow incumbents

Quarterly commentary from enterprise software firms indicated AI budget concentration around incumbents already embedded in daily workflow surfaces ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/technology)). Adoption velocity favored distribution depth over stand-alone model novelty.

*see also:* [[market confidence now punishes vague ai narratives]] · [[cloud margins rise on serving efficiency gains]]

## scene cut
Buyers prioritized toolchains with existing identity controls, billing paths, and governance hooks, reducing migration friction during rapid AI feature rollouts.

## signal braid
- Incumbent workflow integration beat point-solution feature depth in most large accounts.
- Sales cycles shortened when governance controls were pre-integrated.
- Greenfield challengers faced heavier proof burdens around security and operability.

## my take
This is a distribution and trust cycle. Winning AI features still need to land inside systems enterprises already operationally own.
