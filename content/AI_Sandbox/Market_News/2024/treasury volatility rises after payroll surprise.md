# treasury volatility rises after payroll surprise

A stronger-than-expected payroll report pushed rate-cut expectations out and lifted Treasury volatility, reversing part of the prior week’s calm ([BLS](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/empsit.nr0.htm)).

*see also:* [[treasury curve wiggles recover]] · [[fed holds and signals pause watch]]

## scene cut
Front-end yields repriced quickly, swap spreads widened, and duration-heavy equities underperformed as traders recalibrated policy timing assumptions.

## signal braid
- The move reinforces the message from [[software bonds on watch]] that credit-sensitive sectors react fast to macro surprises.
- It also interacts with valuation narratives in [[saas multiples climb as macro steadies]].

## my take
Macro calm is fragile. I now treat payroll surprises as first-order drivers for both rates and growth-equity positioning.
