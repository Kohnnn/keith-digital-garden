# agent platform pricing shifts toward outcome based contracts

Commercial terms for enterprise agent platforms are increasingly tied to workflow completion, error reduction, or cycle-time gains rather than raw usage volume ([Forrester pricing strategy](https://www.forrester.com/)).

*see also:* [[enterprise ai copilot renewals split by realized roi]] · [[knowledge ops tooling consolidates around integrated suites]]

## scene cut
Buyers seek downside protection against underutilized licenses, while vendors try to preserve upside through tiered success metrics.

## signal braid
- Outcome KPIs become central in contract negotiation.
- Measurement disputes emerge around attribution quality.
- Renewals improve where baseline instrumentation is strong.

## my take
Outcome pricing is growing because it aligns incentives, but only when measurement is trustworthy.
