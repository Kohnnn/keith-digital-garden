# S&P 500 Seasonality Breakdown: Q1 Weakness in 2025-W11

**S&P 500 hit correction territory in March 2025, posting Q1's first decline since 2023 amid broken seasonal norms and tariff fears.[1]**

### Q1 Performance Snapshot
- **S&P 500**: -5.63% March, -4.27% Q1; lowest since September.[1]
- **Equal-weight S&P**: -3.38% March, -0.61% Q1â€”hints at broadening beyond mega-caps.[1]
- **DJIA**: -4.06% March, -0.87% Q1; small caps (Russell 2000) -6.81%/-9.48%.[1]
- Nasdaq worst at -8.14%/-10.26%; internationals offset U.S. downside.[1]

### Seasonality Fracture
- March averages +1.1% over 50 years (66% positive), but 2025 deviated sharply.[3][5]
- Early 2025 strength (Jan-Feb) beat norms; unexpected summer gains replaced weakness.[4]
- "Sell in May" muted post-COVID; autumn lows more reliable than spring dips.[2]
- See [[S&P Seasonality Patterns]] for full breaks.

### Key Levels & Signals
- **Support at 5750**: Hold validates summer uptrend; breach signals reversal.[2]
- Short-term: 5830 support, 6100 resistance, neutral at 50-day MA (5998).[3]
- Momentum/breadth weak; Q1 lag questions consumer drive.[1][3]

### Sector/Stock Extremes
- **Winners**: Dollar General +18.54% (defensive appeal), Huntington Ingalls +16.21%.[2]
- **Losers**: Delta Air Lines -27.48% (demand cut), Teradyne -24.81% (trade tensions).[2]
- Airlines hammered by uncertainty; semis wary of tariffs.[2]
