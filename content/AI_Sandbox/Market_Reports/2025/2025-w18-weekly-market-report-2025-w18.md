# 2025-W18 Market Report: UEFN Creator Boom Powers Epic Ecosystem

Fortnite's UEFN surges with AI tools and IP drops, driving creator payouts past $722M amid Epic Store growth[1][2].

## UEFN Tool Expansions
- **AI-powered NPCs** via Persona Device enable dynamic dialogues and gameplay drivers, demoed at Unreal Fest[1][4].
- **Scene Graph Beta** launches June 7 for reusable prefabs, cutting Verse code and bugs[1][2].
- **Brick-by-brick LEGO** building hits June 17, plus Bloom Tycoon template[1][4].
- **General Physics Beta** in Q3; Verse scripting replaces devices for platformers, RPGs[1].

## Creator Economy Metrics
- 260K islands logged **11.2B player hours**; Epic paid **$722M** in engagement payouts since UEFN launch[2].
- New **Creator Portal analytics** (June 2025) turn creators into data-driven publishers[5].
- EGS sees **57% rise** in third-party PC buys, 662M free claims, 77% games at peak CCU[3][4].

## IP and Cross-Platform Plays
- Incoming: **Squid Game** (June 27), **Star Wars**, Avatar; builds on TMNT, Walking Dead[1][4].
- Unity devs access Fortnite platform and economy **starting 2026**.

## Risks in the Surge
- **260K+ islands** risk saturation, hurting discoverability[2].
- Tool betas like Scene Graph may spark instability[1].
- Heavy Epic infra reliance curbs creator independence[[Platform Risks]][2].

> **> note** Pragmatic upside: Payouts and analytics solidify monetization, but skepticism warranted on beta polish and Epic lock-in[[Note Name]].
