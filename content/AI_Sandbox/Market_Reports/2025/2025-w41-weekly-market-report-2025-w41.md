# OKLO Surge Fades: Nuclear Hype Meets Crypto Rotation in W41

**OKLO dominated mid-October 2025 with explosive gains before collapsing, while crypto markets rotated away from altcoins amid speculative doubts.**

## The OKLO Phenomenon

OKLO surged dramatically in mid-October, reaching intraday highs above $190 before retreating sharply[1]. By year-end 2025, the stock settled at $71.88, and as of early 2026 trades at $96.17[1]. The week of October 6–12 captured this inflection point: Barclays positioned OKLO as a **leveraged bet in small modular reactors**, while Wedbush noted a positive market nod with a 15.24% surge on October 13[1]. Technical analysis showed 131.93% gains since a September 9 buy signal, but **pivot tops and MACD sell signals** flagged potential further decline[2].

The fundamentals tell a darker story. Q2 2025 financials reveal a net loss of $24.68M despite $731M in assets; negative ROE (-17.02%) and ROA (-10.46%) signal deep profitability challenges[1]. High volatility post-700% surge combined with strong sell ratings creates a **high-risk environment** despite short-term upside projections of 96% over three months[2].

## Crypto Rotation Underway

Bitcoin held its bull market support band while dominance rose—signaling the **final altcoin rotation is already happening**[3]. Gold broke above $3900, reinforcing precious metals strength[4]. Ethereum, however, dropped 30%, labeled part of a wave of dubious speculation alongside Palladium[5][6]. This divergence reflects capital flight from riskier alts into either hard assets or dominant cryptocurrencies.

## Equity Market Skepticism

The stock market for the week ending October 10 showed no crash—described as "shrewd animal" behavior—yet confidence eroded as **Big Tech faced accusations of faking revenue**[7][8]. This structural doubt undermines the broader equity narrative and suggests selective sector rotation rather than broad-based strength.
