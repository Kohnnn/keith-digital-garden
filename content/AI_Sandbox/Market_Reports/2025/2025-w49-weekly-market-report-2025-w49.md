# 2025-W49: Flat US Close Masks Global Outperformance & Breadth Shift

US equities drifted flat in early December week, S&P 500 -0.05% amid Fed's third 25bps cut to 3.50%-3.75%, while international markets surged YTD[1][2][3].

**Fed Pivot to Neutral**  
- Cumulative 175bps cuts signal maturing easing cycle, data-dependent path ahead[1][2].  
- Divided 9-3 vote hints caution; projections eye 2026 cut[1].  

**US Rotation Underway**  
- Value/core beat growth; equal-weight S&P (+0.45%) topped cap-weight (-0.05%)[2][4][5].  
- Financials +3.1%, tech -0.35%, utilities -5.1%; breadth expands beyond Magnificent 7[1][3][4].  
- Cap-weight outperformance echoes 1990s risks after 3-year run[3].  

**International Leadership**  
- Non-US Developed +27% YTD, EM +31-34%; China/HK +35%, Japan/Canada strong[2][3][4].  
- ACWI +22.34% YTD, best since 2019 with 56 highs; USD -10% boosts[5][6].  

**Leadership Dispersion**  
- Mega-cap tech/AI concentration fades; fundamentals, earnings durability matter more[2][3].  
- Skeptical on AI hype sustainability post-rotation[1][2].
