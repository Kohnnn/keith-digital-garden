Rivian’s IPO surge was a test of EV valuation logic. The company arrived with real products but limited deliveries, and the market priced it as a future giant. That gap between narrative and volume was the story.

I read it as a capital signal. When markets assign massive value to early-stage production, it tells the rest of the sector that growth is the product. <span class="inline-claim">Valuation is a policy signal for capital allocation</span>.

The risk is execution. IPO momentum is a launch window, not a guarantee. The real test is delivering at scale without losing margin or trust.

## signals
- EV valuation premiums remained extreme in late 2021.
- Markets rewarded future capacity more than current output.
- Capital followed brand and narrative as much as metrics.
- Execution risk became the hidden discount.
- Competition pressure rose as capital flooded in.

## my take
This was a moment of peak optimism. It doesn’t mean the company won’t deliver, but it does mean the bar is set unusually high. The market priced a lot of the future into day one.
