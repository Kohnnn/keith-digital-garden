This interactive breakdown of a mechanical watch made complex mechanics readable. It is a reminder that good interfaces can teach by reducing cognitive load.

I read it as a UX lesson for technical education. <span class="inline-claim">Clarity is often just good visual sequencing</span>.

> [!note] Core claim
> Interactive explanations can turn complex systems into intuitive ones.

> [!question] Reflective question
> What other technical domains need this level of visual clarity?

## signals
- Interactive education works when visuals mirror the system.
- Long-form web experiences still have leverage.
- Tooling can be a narrative device.
- Craft is visible in the pacing of information.

## my take
The best technical content is often interface design. This kind of work sets a higher bar for teaching complex systems.

<ul class="brain-dump">
  <li><strong>Interface:</strong> Education improves with better sequencing.</li>
