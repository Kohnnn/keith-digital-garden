Pockit showcased a tiny modular computer built around extendable components. It felt like a reminder that small form factors can still be flexible if the interfaces are clean.

I read it as a hardware craft signal. <span class="inline-note">Modularity scales down when the connectors are right</span>.

> [!note] Core claim
> Modular design can survive even when devices shrink.

> [!question] Reflective question
> How much modularity do users actually want in pocket devices?

## signals
- Modularity remains attractive in constrained hardware.
- DIY ecosystems still drive innovation.
- Small devices can still be repairable.
- Interface design decides extensibility.

## my take
This is a reminder that hardware innovation can be small and still meaningful. It signals that repairability and expansion are still valued.
