# open weights changed what moats mean

Open weights did not kill defensibility; they relocated it. In 2024, the durable moat shifted from private model access to distribution, workflow depth, and governance reliability.

*see also:* [[meta releases llama 2 weight download]] · [[open source model audits become procurement baseline]]

## context plus claim
When capable base models are broadly available, product differentiation moves upward in the stack. The real question becomes who can convert model capability into dependable outcomes inside existing work.

## counter model worth taking seriously
The counter argument says closed model leaders still dominate because frontier quality remains expensive. That is partly true, but it misses a market fact: most enterprise tasks reward reliability and control more than marginal benchmark gains.

## decision boundary
If your customer base values strict control, open weights plus strong governance can beat closed APIs. If your domain demands cutting-edge reasoning at all times, closed providers retain edge for now.

## my take
Open weights made competition healthier. Teams now win by shipping better systems, not by renting mystery boxes and calling it strategy.
