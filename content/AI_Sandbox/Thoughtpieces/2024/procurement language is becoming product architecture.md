# procurement language is becoming product architecture

Enterprise contracts now routinely specify auditability, data handling, and rollback obligations that directly shape system architecture choices ([ISO 42001 overview](https://www.iso.org/standard/81230.html)).

*see also:* [[ai safety evals move into procurement checklists]] · [[cloud software guidance favors workflow incumbents]]

## contract as design input
When indemnity and compliance clauses tighten, teams change data flows, logging patterns, and deployment topology to satisfy nonfunctional guarantees.

## structural impact
- Architecture decisions shift earlier into procurement cycles.
- Vendor shortlists narrow to providers with evidence ready controls.
- Product teams inherit obligations encoded by legal terms.

## boundary condition
This creates value when contract obligations map to measurable runtime behavior.

## my take
Procurement is no longer a downstream step. It is an upstream architecture author.
