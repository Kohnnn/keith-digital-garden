# compliance by design is finally becoming cheaper than rework

Organizations are finding that early control integration reduces total lifecycle cost compared with repeated late-stage fixes under tighter governance expectations ([ISO 27001 overview](https://www.iso.org/isoiec-27001-information-security.html)).

*see also:* [[model governance now lives in release engineering]] · [[compliance automation vendors bundle assurance with deployment]]

## economics of delay
Delayed compliance creates release bottlenecks, audit debt, and costly retrofit cycles that compound with scale.

## what is changing
- Control requirements are now codified earlier in roadmaps.
- Teams quantify remediation cost in quarterly planning.
- Governance automation gets direct budget justification.

## my take
Compliance by design is no longer ideology; it is pragmatic cost control.
