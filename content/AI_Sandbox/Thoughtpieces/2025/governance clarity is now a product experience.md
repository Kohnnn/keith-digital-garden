# governance clarity is now a product experience

Governance quality is becoming visible to end users through explanation quality, override pathways, and predictable refusal behavior, not only internal compliance reports ([Nielsen Norman Group](https://www.nngroup.com/)).

*see also:* [[latency targets are now product promises not infra metrics]] · [[safety claims without eval lineage are just marketing]]

## experience shift
Users now perceive governance through the product surface, especially when systems decline actions or request confirmation.

## practical implication
- Transparency quality affects trust retention.
- Inconsistent override behavior feels like product unreliability.
- Governance UX becomes a competitive differentiator.

## my take
Governance is no longer hidden infrastructure; it is part of the product contract users feel.
