# incident disclosure quality is becoming a strategic asset

Buyers and regulators are rewarding organizations that disclose incidents with clarity on causes, impact boundaries, and remediation timelines ([CISA incident response](https://www.cisa.gov/resources-tools/services/incident-response)).

*see also:* [[the biggest ai moat is now incident memory]] · [[reliability theater collapses under transparent metrics]]

## why disclosure matters
Disclosure quality signals operational competence and accountability under pressure.

## strategic effects
- Transparent vendors recover trust faster after incidents.
- Poor disclosures extend commercial and legal fallout.
- Renewal decisions increasingly incorporate disclosure history.

## my take
Disclosure quality is turning incident management into a visible competitive signal.
