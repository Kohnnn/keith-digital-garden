# agent handoff protocols reduce enterprise escalation risk

Teams are standardizing explicit handoff boundaries between autonomous actions and human approval to avoid silent failure propagation in enterprise workflows ([NIST AI RMF](https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework)).

*see also:* [[stateful agents gain safer rollback controls]] · [[enterprise ai roadmaps fail where ownership is ambiguous]]

## why handoffs fail
Escalations often break when agents pass partial context, unclear confidence, or unstructured action history to humans.

## operating signal
- Incident recovery improves when handoff packets are structured.
- Approval latency decreases with concise evidence summaries.
- Escalation loops shrink when fallback ownership is explicit.

## my take
Reliability comes from transitions, not only model quality.
