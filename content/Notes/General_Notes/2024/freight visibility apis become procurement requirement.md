# freight visibility apis become procurement requirement

Large shippers in 2024 increasingly required API-level freight visibility in carrier contracts, turning tracking from optional feature into procurement baseline ([Drewry](https://www.drewry.co.uk/)).

*see also:* [[red sea shipping shocks keep premiums elevated]] · [[global airfreight index rebounds amid e-commerce]]

## what changed in contracts
RFPs now include event granularity requirements, webhook SLAs, and incident notification windows. Logistics buyers want machine-readable truth, not PDF status updates.

## signal braid
- Visibility APIs reduce reaction time during route disruptions.
- They improve inventory planning when schedules slip unexpectedly.
- They also expose carriers with weak data maturity.

## risk surface
- Data quality varies by corridor and partner integration depth.
- Over-automated exception handling can misroute decisions if upstream data is wrong.
- Smaller carriers risk exclusion if integration costs remain high.

## my take
This is a healthy shift. If logistics is digital, contract language should be digital too.
