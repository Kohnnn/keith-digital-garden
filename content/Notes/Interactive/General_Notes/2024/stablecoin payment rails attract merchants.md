# stablecoin payment rails attract merchants

Retail and B2B merchants expanded stablecoin settlement pilots in 2024 to reduce card and remittance fees, especially in cross-border flows ([CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/business/2024/07/03/stablecoin-payments-merchant-growth/)). The interesting shift is operational: finance teams now test crypto rails as treasury tooling, not speculative exposure.

*see also:* [[bitcoin etf approvals change treasury playbooks]] · [[otc crypto clearing nudge reduces counterparty risk]]

## context + claim
The value proposition is speed and predictability. If settlement finality improves cash conversion cycles, merchants tolerate integration complexity.

## evidence stack
- Pilots concentrate in gaming, creator payouts, and export-heavy SMBs.
- Treasury teams favor fully collateralized instruments over algorithmic variants.
- Compliance workflows are being redesigned around wallet screening and transaction monitoring.

## risk surface
- Counterparty and issuer concentration can reintroduce hidden fragility.
- Jurisdictional treatment remains inconsistent, complicating accounting.
- Customer support burdens rise when payment rails are unfamiliar.

## my take
Stablecoin adoption now looks boring in the best way: practical, fee-driven, and operationally focused. That is a healthier growth signal than hype cycles.
