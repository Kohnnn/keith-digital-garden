# agent approval ladders replace binary human in the loop

Teams are adopting multi-step approval ladders where action risk determines review depth, replacing simple yes-or-no handoff gates ([NIST trustworthy ai](https://www.nist.gov/programs-projects/trustworthy-and-responsible-ai)).

*see also:* [[agent handoff protocols reduce enterprise escalation risk]] · [[agent override protocols become training requirement for managers]]

## control model
Low-risk actions proceed with lightweight oversight, while high-risk actions require staged approvals and stronger audit evidence.

## operating signal
- Escalation quality improves with clearer decision tiers.
- Reviewer fatigue declines when trivial requests are filtered.
- Audit narratives become more coherent across teams.

## my take
Approval ladders make autonomy governance more proportional and less brittle.
