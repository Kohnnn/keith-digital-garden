# retrieval quality is becoming a political question inside firms

As enterprise retrieval systems become central to decision workflows, teams are discovering that ownership boundaries and curation priorities can outweigh model choices ([Harvard Business Review](https://hbr.org/topic/technology)).

*see also:* [[enterprise knowledge graphs return as retrieval control plane]] · [[enterprise rag failure modes cluster in stale corpora]]

## where politics enters
The question of whose documents are trusted, refreshed, and prioritized directly shapes answer quality and perceived fairness.

## structural outcomes
- Retrieval disputes appear as governance conflicts.
- Data ownership clarity predicts answer reliability.
- Underfunded domains become invisible in AI outputs.

## my take
Retrieval quality is now part information architecture and part organizational power map.
