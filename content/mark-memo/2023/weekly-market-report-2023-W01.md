The first week of 2023 traded like a relief reset, not a durable regime change.

I treated this week like a negotiation between policy pressure and positioning fatigue.

## Overall Summary
- US payrolls rose by 223k while unemployment fell to 3.5%.
- US 2Y yields stayed above 4.2%, signaling policy remained restrictive.
- BTC held above $16.5k but still traded like macro beta.

## Commentary
- I gave more weight to what persisted for 2-3 sessions than to single-day headline reactions.
- I avoided broad conviction until breadth and rate volatility improved together.
- I treated every strong move as provisional until the next macro catalyst cleared.

## Rates & Liquidity
- Front-end yields remained the fastest transmission channel for macro repricing.
- Liquidity quality stayed uneven around event clusters.

## Equities
- Index direction still masked meaningful leadership and breadth dispersion.
- I favored durability and cash-flow quality over narrative beta.

## Commodities & FX
- Dollar direction continued to mediate global risk appetite.
- Commodity behavior stayed a high-signal input for inflation confidence.

## Crypto Macro
- BTC remained macro-coupled through rates and liquidity expectations.
- I watched spot participation versus leverage impulse before trusting breakouts.

## Positioning & Flow
- Flow looked tactical with frequent squeeze-then-fade patterns.
- I kept exposure modular and cut quickly on failed follow-through.
