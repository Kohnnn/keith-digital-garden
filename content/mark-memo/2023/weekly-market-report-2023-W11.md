Banking stress week: stability risk overtook inflation as the first-order market driver.

## Overall Summary
- SVB and Signature failures reset cross-asset risk behavior.
- Emergency policy backstops changed liquidity expectations quickly.
- BTC outperformed during the stability panic as policy-liquidity bets rose.

## Commentary
- I prioritized rates translation and breadth quality over headline velocity.
- I upgraded exposure only when follow-through survived the next macro test.
- I treated failed breakouts as risk-management signals.

## Rates & Liquidity
- Front-end rates remained the fastest policy transmission channel.
- Liquidity quality stayed event-driven and regime-sensitive.

## Equities
- Index-level strength often masked concentration and fragile internals.
- I favored resilience and balance-sheet quality over narrative beta.

## Commodities & FX
- Dollar and commodity behavior remained key cross-asset stress gauges.
- FX shifts helped validate or reject short-horizon risk narratives.

## Crypto Macro
- BTC stayed highly coupled to macro liquidity and policy expectations.
- I watched spot participation versus leverage impulse before trusting continuation.

## Positioning & Flow
- Positioning looked tactical with frequent squeeze-then-fade behavior.
- I kept gross risk modular and reduced quickly when correlation spikes returned.
