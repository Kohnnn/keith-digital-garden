## Overall Summary
- Macro surprises shifted from inflation fear toward slowdown interpretation.
- Lower yields supported broader sector participation.
- BTC benefited as real-rate pressure moderated.

## Regime Read
I approached this week as a chain reaction, not a set of disconnected headlines. My process was simple: identify the first mover, test whether other markets confirmed it, and only then adjust exposure. That framework prevented me from overreacting to single-session noise.

## Term Premium and Rate Pressure
Rates were still the first filter. When repricing was orderly, I could hold risk with wider stops; when repricing became disorderly, I shortened holding periods and treated upside as tactical. That distinction decided most of my wins and losses this week.

## Decision Boundary
- I add risk only when rates, breadth, and flow point in the same direction.
- I cut size when headline momentum outruns participation quality.
- I treat single-catalyst breakouts as tactical until cross-asset confirmation appears.

## Where I Was Wrong
- I was early on at least one move and had to wait for confirmation that came later than expected.
- I underestimated how quickly positioning can flip when rates volatility compresses.
- I can improve by writing invalidation levels before entries, not after.

## Outlook Next Week
- Main risk: A single hot inflation print can re-open higher-for-longer stress.
- Main opportunity: Continued cooling in labor and inflation can extend broad risk recovery.
