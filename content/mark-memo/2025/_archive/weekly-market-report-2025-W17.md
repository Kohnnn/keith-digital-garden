# Weekly Market Report 2025-W17

This week carried a clearer risk signal: gold broke $3,300 and BTC dominance hit a new cycle high. Those two signals told me the risk premium stayed elevated. The equity tape was active, but the cross‑asset message was defensive.

I kept cross-checking with [[weekly-market-report-2025-W16]] because the post-drawdown fragility did not go away. The difference was that safe‑haven demand and dominance metrics became louder. When gold runs and dominance rises together, I take it as a warning that liquidity remains cautious.

The crypto narrative also leaned defensive with death-cross chatter. That doesn’t guarantee a breakdown, but it does increase the odds of reflexive selling on any macro shock.

## Overall Summary
This was a risk‑aware week. Gold’s break above $3,300 and dominance highs both pointed to caution. The equity tape moved, but it didn’t erase the cross‑asset warning signals.

The takeaway for me is that risk appetite remains conditional. The market may be recovering, but it is doing so under the shadow of defensive positioning.

I also noticed how quickly traders reverted to hedging behavior after any intraday strength. That tells me the bid is still nervous. When the market is confident, it lets winners run; when it is cautious, it sells into strength. This week felt like the latter.

- Gold broke $3,300, a clear risk‑premium signal.
- BTC dominance hit a new cycle high, reinforcing concentration.
- Death‑cross chatter kept crypto sentiment fragile.

## Commentary
I treated this as confirmation that the risk premium stayed high. A strong gold bid is not a bullish signal; it is a hedging signal. Pair that with dominance highs, and you get a market that is not ready to broaden.

I also noted the “death cross rally” framing. That type of rally can be sharp, but it is usually fragile. It keeps me in a defensive posture.

My own rule in this regime is to respect the hedges more than the headlines. If gold is running and BTC dominance is rising, I assume liquidity is still concentrated and that any rally is a relief rally, not a rotation.

- I stayed cautious because defensive signals were loud.
- I treated rallies as tactical, not structural.

## Rates & Liquidity
Rates were not the main story. Liquidity cues came from gold and dominance instead. That tells me the market is still managing risk rather than adding it.

In practice, that means I keep a shorter time horizon and focus on confirmation rather than anticipation. I do not want to build a thesis that depends on the market broadening while the hedging signals remain loud.

- Liquidity signals came from cross‑asset hedges, not rates.
- Risk appetite remained constrained.

## Equities
Equities were active but not convincing. I did not see broad participation that would contradict the defensive cross‑asset signals.

When I zoomed out, the price action looked more like a stabilization attempt than a trend. That is fine, but it means I still need evidence of improving breadth before I increase risk.

- Equity strength stayed selective.
- Breadth remained limited.

## Commodities & FX
Gold’s move was the headline. The $3,300 break is a strong signal that hedging demand remains high. I treated it as confirmation of cautious sentiment.

- Gold strength reinforced the risk‑off bias.
- FX remained secondary to the gold signal.

## Crypto Macro
Dominance hit a new cycle high, and death‑cross chatter rose. That combination keeps me defensive. It suggests the market is concentrated and sentiment is fragile.

- Dominance at cycle highs kept the market BTC‑heavy.
- Death‑cross chatter raised the risk of reflexive selling.

## Positioning & Flow
Flows remained tactical. I did not see accumulation that would argue for a durable trend. That matches the defensive cross‑asset signals.

- Positioning stayed cautious.
- I avoided chasing rallies.

## Outlook Next Week
Next week is about whether gold and dominance cool or intensify. If they cool, risk can stabilize. If they intensify, the risk premium will stay high.
